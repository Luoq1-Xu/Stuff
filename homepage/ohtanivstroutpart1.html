The Backstory

If you don't already know who these two are, they are two of the best baseball players currently active. Think Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo, if they played on the same mediocre team. I digress. Now, bottom of the ninth, Finals of the World Baseball Classic, 2 outs, Japan leading 3-2 against the USA, Shohei Ohtani for Japan vs Mike Trout for the USA. If you don't understand what that means, think finals of the FIFA World Cup, 90th minute, one team leading 1-0, penalty for the team that is behind. Veeery high presure situation.


Now, even if trout hits a homerun (best possible outcome), the score will still be tied 3-3. However, the beauty of baseball is that anything can happen. If trout wins this at bat, the momentum shifts massively and the USA has a real chance of making a comeback. Japan and the USA have been undoubtedly the two best teams in this tournament, and neither team deserved to lose.

The At bat
Pitch 1 : An 88 mph slider with good movement.

Good movement, but trout doesn't Bite. Ball 1.

Count : 1 - 0


Pitch 2 : A 100! mph fastball

right down the middle.



One thing you need to know about Trout is that he is realllly good at hitting fastballs.


A swing and a miss. Strike 1.

Count : 1 - 1


Pitch 3 : A 100mph Fastball on the outer corner.

Juuuuust a little bit outside. The ball cuts, meaning that it moves slightly outside. Really good take by trout there. It would have been difficult to do damage on a fastball low and outside, especially one with such velocity. The pitch also looked like it could have clipped the strike zone.


Count : 2 - 1
